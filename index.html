Who We Are

Born from shared experience. Built for the whole church.

Accents is a ministry led by four immigrant pastors who have each navigated the unique joys and challenges of planting and leading new worshiping communities within the PCUSA.

We identified a persistent gap: immigrant congregations full of life and energy, often disconnected from the broader denominational family. Mid council leaders and chartered churches eager to engage, unsure how to begin. Accents exists to close that gap.

Through resources, conferences, regional gatherings, and one-on-one coaching, we build the relational and structural bridges that allow immigrant communities and established congregations to truly flourish together.

Our Work

How We Serve

Three interconnected pillars that move communities from isolation to deep, lasting partnership.

🌱

Equip Immigrant Leaders

We provide coaching, training, and peer community for immigrant pastors leading New Worshiping Communities — helping them navigate denominational systems and grow healthy congregations.

🤝

Build Healthy Partnerships

We guide mid council leaders and chartered congregations through the practical and relational work of becoming genuine partners — not just sponsors — to immigrant communities.

🗺️

Gather the Community

Our regional gatherings and annual conferences create space for immigrant leaders and their allies to meet, share, worship, and strengthen the bonds that sustain long-term ministry.

Who We Serve

Three Communities. One Vision.

NWCs

Immigrant New Worshiping Communities

Congregations led by immigrant pastors within the PCUSA, often spanning multiple languages and cultures, bringing renewal and vitality to the church.

MCLs

Mid Council Leaders

Presbytery and synod leaders who want to support immigrant NWCs well — providing oversight, resources, and denominational connection without overreach.

CCs

Chartered Congregations

Established PCUSA churches exploring what it means to be genuine partners — offering space, resources, and relationship — while learning from immigrant communities.

The Partnership Model

From sponsor to sibling.

Healthy partnerships between immigrant NWCs and established congregations don't happen by accident. They require intention, humility, and a clear framework. Accents walks every party through this process together.

Start the Conversation
1

Listen & Learn

Mid council leaders and chartered churches begin by listening — understanding the culture, language, and vision of the immigrant community.

2

Build Relationship

Shared meals, worship exchanges, and facilitated dialogue move the relationship from transactional to genuine friendship.

3

Define Partnership

Accents helps all parties create a clear, written covenant that outlines mutual commitments, expectations, and shared goals.

4

Sustain & Renew

We provide ongoing support and check-ins to help partnerships navigate challenges and continue growing together.
